Agroecological Cultural Precincts (ACPs), Research proposal work period: 2017-ongoing

How to coexist harmoniously with the other, be that person, animal, plant or landscape, so that each can find a space for self-realization through a mutually enriching coexistence? This is an interdisciplinary project that combines Arts and Agroecology to explore the wide range of possibilities to relate to the environment sustainably.


ACP project case-study 1: Initial explorations in clay and fibers at Ogród Salwatore, 2019-2021

Without any external tools or materials, but exclusively those from the place, three type of installations were created using clay, leaves and fibers found in-situ. Clay on trees: "The Tree Indian", "The imperial chinese man tree", "Astra, the dog in tree", "The frontal tree man". Rope from fibers: "Tree connection". Useful trees: "The Gym tree" (accomodated to facilitate several calisthenic exercises. Not visible on the pictures). I authored the previously mentioned, and contributed to the "The temple trees".

Acción Ecológica, 2017

Organized a group named: “Ecological Action”, which consisted in a series of walks for about a year (10 events in total) into beautiful but polluted natural areas to collect the garbage found and make an artistic act in the cleaned areas. All the participants contributed actively in every stage: Garbage collection, Art (usually Music with three guitars, flutes, and percussion) and ideas concerning the environment, arts and sustainability which were discussed during the picnic we always made after the intense cleaning. The city office collaborated with our initiative by providing containers. Children also participated enthusiastically and it was educational and recreative for all participants.



Dialogical Artifacts Project, 2015

Collaborated with this project by filming, and helping in the design and testing of some of the dialogical artifacts. They consist of sculptures and artistic installations that produced sound from human and natural sources of action, such as the wind, water, sun, gravity, etc.

Short films in collaboration with Francisco Huichaqueo, 2011

Produced two short films in collaboration with Francisco Huichaqueo, a Mapuche film-director. The first one titled "El Ronco" explores the moral perspective of a man that has recently been liberated from prison, where he has spent most of his life.

The second one, titled "El Muro" attempts to rediscover the commonly-ignored textures of the walls that surround us.
